Blockchain and Crypto currencies

by Jarrod MurphyOct 15th,  2021

Blockchain, much like the name is a chain
of blocks containing information or data. The technology was originally used to
timestamp digital documents so that it's not possible to backdate them or to
tamper with them, this went mostly unused though. The increase in interest in
blockchain technology came when the creator of bitcoin “Satoshi Nakamoto”
(which is a pseudonym) integrated blockchain into cryptocurrency when bitcoin
was created in 2009. A block chain is a distributed ledger that is completely
open to anyone. Once some data has been recorded inside the blockchain it
becomes exceedingly difficult to change it. The security from the blockchain
comes from its creative use of hashing which is when a transaction has been
verified and needs to be added to a block in the chain, it will be put through
a hash algorithm to convert it into a set of unique numbers and letters, like
what would be created by a password generator. The proof of work protocol which
is used by cryptocurrencies to validate the transactions that occur in their
networks. Also adding to the security of the blockchain is that it is
distributed over a Peer-to-Peer network (P2P) this means that even if one peer
goes down, the other peers are still present in the network. Therefore, cryptocurrencies
of all types make use of blockchain technology, Blockchains act as a
decentralized system for recording and documenting transactions. This
technology in the future could be used for many different things that need to
be used securely and efficiently to transfer user data across platforms and
systems. The technology could be sued to maintain and protect things like
records of real estate ownership, titles and more.

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual
currency that is meant to be a medium of exchange. Cryptocurrency works much
like normal currency without the physical embodiment of money. It uses
cryptography and blockchain technology to record every transaction and ledger.
It is a decentralised system run over a P2P network to optimise security so
everyone on the network verifies the ledgers and transactions so that is hard
to tamper with the data. Cryptocurrency in the future could be used in
countries with very unstable economies. This would help the people in these
countries have a more stable financial environment. Cryptocurrency could also
bring a low-cost, high-speed way of making payments that could change the way
we exchange money and use banks, similarly to how the internet transformed the
way information was exchanged cryptocurrency could bring a revolution to the
way everyone regards money and banks.

For me personally I'm interested in this
technology as I've invested money into cryptocurrencies when there's dips in
the price and use it in the same way as you can with the stock market. Most of
my family and friends are usually overwhelmed with the explanation of this and
do not understand the concepts and technologies behind it which gets rather
frustrating and unfortunate. In my own opinion this could be rectified with simpler
ways of using and explaining the technology and methods you use to buy and sell
cryptocurrencies and use blockchain technology.

Cloud Services and Servers

by NGO KHANH TUNGOct 15th,  2021

Many see the Internet as websites, software, and services where
they can perform their activities, finding information, gaming or even
socializing. But the Internet is much more than that. How about creating a
website, writing a software, compute a set of data or storing data? A term
‘Cloud Computing’ is born to indicate the activities of transferring data using
the Internet. It’s a broad term that describe almost any Internet activities.
Since the Internet is vast by its constantly development and complexity, the
following paragraphs will mention the importance and common uses of Cloud
Computing.

Chellappa first used the term “Cloud Computing” with the ideas
focusing on how cloud services can greatly impact small and medium-sized
enterprises and later business models [1]. Clouds services offer a wide range of
configurable computing resources which are excellent for their flexibility
and scalability to meet most of the business’ models’ requirements and the
criteria from the users with minimal management effort [2].

Regarding the future uses of cloud computing, it is certainly the
most required for Internet of Things. Steering a bit to imagine how
self-driving cars work, the “AI” of the car will decide how the car runs on the
street just like us driving a car. All the cars that can measure the parameters
of the surroundings like temperature, humidity, traffics, route can be shared
with each other via the Internet, or the Cloud Computing we are mentioning. The
synchronization of the vehicles on the street makes it even possible to
eliminate the abundant needs for traffic lights. The cars accidents and traffic
congestion are expected to be significantly minimized at that point. These good
things will be followed by even more, like CO2 emission, lower fuel
consumption, less time spent on the street. The present of Internet’s services,
it should be more rapid to install and apply new software/firmware to the AIs
inside driving systems instead of physically dealing with the cars one by one
[3].

The existence of Cloud Computing makes many companies, especially
traditional ones shifting from being local to being on Cloud. The cost for
maintaining hardware storages, locations, local policies come from building a
local server. Cloud Computing provides means of computation, storage for many
organizations which help them being more fucus on other aspects of business
with less concerns on data, securities or even cost. Amazon Web Services (AWS)
and Google Cloud (GC) are very scalable and on-premises, shifting from high
computation power or high volume of storage capacity to lower ones and vice
versa are a matter of buttons. They even have tools for shifting and predicting
the needs of compute power and storage, like AWS Elastic Beanstalk.

With the power of a very recent technology, Quantum Computing,
a new field of Cloud technology is now opened. The quantum computers are now
implemented to be accessible via cloud services, users have never been closer
to the new power of quantum processing. Big cloud service companies have made
their ways to deliver such technologies on their platforms. IBM, Microsoft,
Google and Amazon have implemented such technology and continue to
develop further to make quantum computing more approachable for users. The
quantum computers can be harnessed by utilizing cloud computing without
physically having a quantum computer [4]. Even many have been able to reach
this very latest technology, not many impacts have been made since this way of
computing is still new and not very viable compared to the decades-worth
developments of traditional bit computing, and most of the activities are
around researching and testing. This is even worried by its compatibility with
other devices and technologies that we have already developed. By getting
closer to quantum computing by combining cloud computing, the development of
new quantum-based technologies will undoubtably skyrocket and greatly impact
not only computer science but other branches like medical, infrastructure,
transportation. It is still unknown how the quantum computing and cloud
computing will take us; however, with later years of great innovations and
developments, these new technologies will surely change the way we know how to
use a computer in the future.

The presence of cloud computing has great impact on the employees.
The shifting from local network infrastructure to cloud-based systems across
companies has been seen in the recent years. Almost 70% of organizations are
utilizing cloud services to cope the rising issues in the years of pandemic,
indicated by a Gartner survey in November [5]. A majority of companies prefer
cloud services because of their benefits including flexibility, scalability,
security, efficiency and possible cost reduction. This greatly impact the
workforce inside the IT industry. The needs for local storage and networks
which are required in many organizations will still exist but receive a great
reduction. This indicates the needs for network architect/developer will be
lower since less installation and maintenance are required. New job titles have
appeared recently to handle cloud services.

Autonomous vehicles

by JOHN NORBERGOct 15th,  2021

Autonomous vehicles are
vehicles which can be driven autonomously by an Artificial Intelligence (AI).
Most people will be familiar with self-driving cars, but the concept of
autonomous vehicles can be applied to any vehicle, such as tractors, airplanes,
and buses. [1] The basic principle for what makes an autonomous vehicle an
autonomous vehicle is that is it a vehicle which can fulfill its purpose
without any active human intervention, which is accomplished by allowing the
vehicle to sense its surroundings and have an AI make decisions. This will have
the possibility to revolutionize a huge variety of different fields such as
agriculture (self-driving tractors), public transportation (self-driving taxis,
self-driving buses etc), transportation of goods (self-driving trucks) and can
be a major help for people with disabilities since they will be able to be more
independent with the use of self-driving cars. However, there is also a large
array of various dangers with autonomous vehicles such as ethical concerns (AI
does not have a conscience), legal issues (who is at fault if an accident
occurs involving autonomous vehicles) and issues regarding military use.

Because the term
“autonomous vehicle” is so broad, different “levels” have been defined to
clearly explain how advanced/intrusive the “autonomous” part is. There are 6
defined and accepted levels, these being (in order 0-5) no automation, driver
assistance, partial automation, conditional automation, high automation, and
full automation. So, a regular manual car would be classified as a level 0
autonomous vehicles while a fully self-driving car (one without a human driver)
would be a level 5. [3] Currently most car manufacturers are focused on the

Machine Learning

by ABDUL-HALIMOct 15th,  2021

In the near future, the self-learning artificial
intelligence system Skynet becomes self-aware. It considers humanity a threat
to its existence, and begins the extermination of the human race. This plot
from the Terminator films is what some people think of Machine Learning. But
what is Machine Learning really?

First, let’s take a look at how normal
computation is done. This is, in itself, a mystery to many people; it almost
seems like magic. Computing seems improbable; from lifeless materials, we have
managed to make a machine that can almost ‘think’. The secret to computers is
in the control of electrons, which is traced back to the incredible invention
of electricity (but we won’t go back that far). Computers calculate by a
special configuration of wires and circuits, accompanied by another great
invention of the 20th century: the transistor. Transistors work like
switches in the circuit and are made of (primarily silicon) what’s known as a
semiconductor, which can switch states between conducting and not conducting
electricity, allowing it to turn the flow of electricity on or off. This ‘on’
or ‘off’ state of electricity is represented as binary; 1 or 0. Amazingly, by
arranging just these two states of information in complex configurations, has
the potential to make complex calculations. Dealing with two states of
information, or Booleans, such as true or false, already had an established
area of mathematics, known as Boolean Algebra. The three basic operations in
Boolean Algebra are the NOT, AND, OR operations. The various Boolean operations
could be replicated by arranging the transistor circuits in specific
arrangements, known as ‘gates’. By the precise addition of more specific logic
gates, a circuit can be made to be able to calculate addition, and many more
complex functions.

Using programming, we can instruct computers to
perform many functions. However, the basic function of being told ‘If this,
then that’ is not enough for some more complex problems. That’s where Machine
Learning comes in. Machine Learning allows computers to analyse data and make their own decisions regarding it. Machine Learning is
an application of Artificial Intelligence; the concept of making computers make
human-like decisions. Machine Learning is a system that can learn from
experience and make improvements automatically. Here’s an example of Machine
Learning: let’s say we’re trying to make a program that can identify pictures
of cats and dogs. This is a simple task for humans, even very small children,
but it’s a bit more complicated for a machine. The way we ‘teach’ the machine
is similar to the evolutionary process of mutation and natural selection. A
random set of algorithms are created, and each are tested on distinguishing the
pictures of cats and dogs. Humans provide the test data with the answer key, in
this example, pictures of cats and pictures of dogs. The algorithms decisions
are marked according the answer key, or labeled data automatically, by the
human programmed testing bot. At first, you would end up with a bunch of
entirely random algorithms, some more successful than others. The most
successful algorithm survives, it and random variations of it are tested again.
Again, the most successful survives, and this continues until you get to a
point where the algorithm is accurate enough to tell apart the cats and the
dogs.

The term Machine Learning was first coined in
1952, where Arthur Samuel of IBM described a learning process in a computer
program for Checkers. At first, Arthur had created a system that analysed
Checkers positions and determined which side had a better chance of winning. He
developed ways to make the computer ‘learn’, by having the program remember the
positions it had already seen and applying a reward system. In 1997, a computer
known as DeepBlue which had trained on thousands of Chess Grandmaster’s games,
defeated the world Chess champion Garry Kasparov. This was the first time a
machine had defeated the world champion human at Chess. Machine Learning in its
current state has been produced to master strategy games such as Go and Chess,
and is being developed to learn to drive. There are many more applications of
Machine Learning, such as picture and audio recognition. In 2017, DeepMind
created AlphaZero, which learnt to play Go and Chess by playing against itself
thousands of times, learning by an AI system known as neural networking.
AlphaZero was powerful enough to defeat the top Chess engine in the world 28
times out of a 100, with NO losses (remaining games were drawn). Machine Learning
is also being used to train self-driving cars, to recognize the road lanes,
other cars, traffic systems, etc. In the present day, Tesla has developed a
self-driving car, which could learn from the data of hundreds of thousands of
cars in real life scenarios. Machine Learning is already used to adapt to you,
such as YouTube recommendation algorithms. Machine Learning companies are eager
for your data, as they can use it for the training process of their algorithms.

In the near future (for real this time), the
field of Machine Learning will see vast progress at an incredible rate. Machine
Learning is an extremely pivotal technology, and it will affect many
industries. By 2027, the global Machine Learning market is predicted to grow to
over 117 Billion dollars; almost 14 times more than it is currently. The future
will no doubt see Machine Learning improved tremendously and used as a
widespread technology, tightly intwined with people’s daily lives (it already
is, as a matter of fact). Machine Learning is a fundamental steppingstone to
many other revolutionary technologies, such as the Autonomous Vehicles and
Robots of today and the future. The introduction of electronic technology
replaced many jobs, as did the industrial revolution long before it. It’s hard
to imagine that the super intelligent systems developed by Machine Learning of
the future would not replace many more. Truck drivers drive long hours on end,
over many days, across the long desolate roads of Australia. In the future, if
Machine Learning is trusted enough, Autonomous Vehicles would have the ability
to replace the job of these drivers, taking control of the trucks themselves.
Perhaps taxis, buses, and other forms of public transport could be driverless.
If Robots could be developed and trained efficiently using Machine Learning,
they could identify objects and perhaps replace many heavy lifting and labour
jobs. It’s said that machines could even replace doctors in the future. Tesla
(perhaps jokingly) revealed that they were developing the ‘Tesla Bot’: “Develop
the next generation of automation, including a general purpose, bi-pedal,
humanoid robot capable of performing tasks that are unsafe, repetitive or
boring. We’re seeking mechanical, electrical, controls and software engineers
to help us leverage our AI expertise beyond our vehicle fleet.” Robots could
use Machine Learning to comprehend human speech and formulate coherent and
logical responses. Perhaps Robots could be created to appear, act and behave as
humans, and hypothetically one day we could be none the wiser.

For now, the human field of technology and
Machine Learning is safe from being taken by the machines. That is, until the
machines themselves learn to make better machines. I think that is a good
reason to study in IT and other technology/computer related fields. The future
with Machine Learning doesn’t have to be all bleak; many exciting opportunities
are opened by it, and humanity can achieve a lot of progress and solve numerous
problems that currently face us. Just don’t give the self-learning machines
control of the nukes.
